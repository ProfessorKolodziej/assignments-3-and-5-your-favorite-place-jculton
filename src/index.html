Bar Harbor, ME

A charming coastal town known for its stunning natural landscapes, vibrant cultural scene, and rich history. Spectacular ocean views, serene lakes, and beautiful gardens offer a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The sunrise from Cadillac Mountain in Acadia National Park is particularly famous, as it's one of the first places in the United States to see the sunrise.

Top Destinations

  1. Acadia National Park

    Located adjacent to Bar Harbor, Acadia National Park offers breathtaking landscapes, from rugged coastlines to forested peaks. It's a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with activities such as hiking, biking, and bird watching.

  2. Mount Desert Island

    The largest island off the coast of Maine, is famed for its rugged natural beauty, encompassing the majority of Acadia National Park with its forested peaks, rocky beaches, and glacier-scoured granite cliffs. This idyllic island offers a diverse landscape that attracts outdoor enthusiasts for hiking, biking, and exploring the scenic vistas of Bar Harbor and beyond.

    Directions | More Information

  3. Ocean Trail

    A stunning 4-mile linear trail that runs along the eastern shore of Mount Desert Island, offering breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean, rocky cliffs, and coastal landscapes. Starting from Sand Beach and ending at Otter Cliff, this relatively easy hike is accessible to visitors of all ages

    Directions | More Information

  4. Frenchman Bay

    Known for its panoramic ocean vistas, vibrant marine life, and the iconic Porcupine Islands that dot its horizon. It serves as a popular spot for sailing, kayaking, and whale watching, offering visitors a serene setting to explore the natural beauty and tranquility of Maine's coastal landscape.

    Directions | More Information

  5. Egg Rock Light

    Guiding mariners since its establishment in 1875, this charming and picturesque lighthouse, with its distinctive white tower and red-roofed keeper's house. It is not open to the public but can be admired from boat tours and certain vantage points along the coast, offering a glimpse into Maine's rich maritime history.

Travel Tips

  • Pack layers and waterproof gear, as Bar Harbor's weather can be unpredictable, ranging from sunny and warm to foggy and cool, even in summer.

  • Rent a bike or car to explore the island's hidden gems, as many of the best sights and trails in and around Bar Harbor are spread out.

  • Try the local seafood, particularly lobster, at one of Bar Harbor's many waterfront restaurants for a taste of Maine's culinary delights

  • Plan to visit Acadia National Park early in the morning or during the week to avoid the crowds and fully enjoy the park's natural beauty

  • Reserve accommodations and popular tours well in advance, especially if you're visiting during the peak season from June to August.
